\# Security Metrics and Executive Reporting



\## Overview

Security metrics help measure SOC effectiveness and provide leadership with visibility into detection and response performance. Tracking key indicators such as MTTD, MTTR, and dwell time enables continuous improvement of security operations.



---



\## Key SOC Metrics



\### Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)



\- Incident Occurrence: 10:00  

\- Detection Time: 12:00  



\*\*MTTD = 2 hours\*\*



---



\### Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)



\- Response Start: 12:00  

\- Containment Complete: 16:00  



\*\*MTTR = 4 hours\*\*



---



\### Dwell Time



\- Initial Compromise: 10:00  

\- Detection: 12:00  



\*\*Dwell Time = 2 hours\*\*



---



\### False Positive Rate (Simulated)



\- Total Alerts: 100  

\- False Positives: 15  



\*\*False Positive Rate = 15%\*\*



---



\## Metrics Assessment



The SOC demonstrates reasonable detection and response capability. However, opportunities exist to reduce false positives and further optimize response automation to improve MTTR.



---



\## Executive Summary



SOC monitoring remains effective in identifying and containing threats within acceptable timeframes. Continued investment in automation, detection tuning, and threat intelligence integration is recommended to further reduce dwell time and analyst workload.



---



\## Recommended Improvements



\- Increase SOAR automation coverage  

\- Tune detection rules to reduce false positives  

\- Enhance threat intelligence integration  

\- Improve analyst response playbooks  

\- Conduct periodic SOC performance reviews  



---



\## Outcome



Security metrics provide leadership visibility into SOC effectiveness and support data-driven improvements to detection and response capabilities.
